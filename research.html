Research Interests

Isogeometric Analysis

Part of my work focusses on mathematical modelling using Isogeometric Analysis. In particular, I mainly focus on the modelling of thin (Kirchhoff-Love) shells using Isogeometric Analysis. My focus in shell modelling is on efficient coupling mesh adapitivity schemes, with main emphasis on (post-)buckling analyses.

Wrinkling of Thin Sheets

My primary focus in the field of computational physics is on the wrinkling of thin membranes, optionally supported by a fluid. My aim is to develop mathematical models (using Isogeometric Analysis) to predict the wrinkling behaviour of thin membranes under complex loading conditions efficiently. Additionally, I focus on the development of methods that do not require a-priori perturbation of the sheet.

Related publications

Proceedings of the 12th European Conference on Numerical Mathematics and Advanced Applications (ENUMATH), 2019, Egmond aan Zee (to appear)

Large, Thin, Floating Structures

My work on the modelling of wrinkling in thin membranes supported by fluids is inspured by Very Large Flexible Floating Structures (VLFFSs) used for offshore solar energy production. These platforms are being discussed in academic literature and the offshore energy sector as a potential energy source at sea. One possibility for the design of these platforms is a very thin membrane that deforms with the waves of the sea. Implications, however, related to this design are mooring system design and the wrinkling phenomenon near point loads or stiffness jumps.
